Tuesday, April 07, 2020

It's been a while since we published this blog post. Some of the information may be outdated (for example, some images may be missing, and some links may not work anymore). For information on upcoming events, check out our new [Search Central Live event series](/search/events/search-central-live).

As a part of the [Webmaster Conference series](/search/events/search-central-live),
last fall we held a [Product Summit](/search/blog/2019/09/join-us-at-webmaster-conference-in)
at Google's headquarters in Mountain View, California. It was slightly different from our
previous events, with a number of product managers and engineers from Google Search taking part.
We recorded the talks held there, and are happy to be able to make these available to all of you
now.

In the
[playlist](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLKoqnv2vTMUO0F7xnv1MR2n-n7Y2CFgpR)
you'll find:

* [Web deduplication](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LsfIlviyDvE&list=PLKoqnv2vTMUO0F7xnv1MR2n-n7Y2CFgpR&index=2)
  - How does Google recognize duplicate content across the web? What happens once a duplicate is
  found? How is a canonical URL selected? How does localization play a role?
* [Google Images best practices](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kYwo10wBd_s&list=PLKoqnv2vTMUO0F7xnv1MR2n-n7Y2CFgpR&index=3)
  - Take a look at how Google Images has evolved over the years, and learn about some of the best
  practices that you can implement on your site when it comes to images.
* [Rendering](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rq8sFkl0KnI&list=PLKoqnv2vTMUO0F7xnv1MR2n-n7Y2CFgpR&index=4)
  - Find out more about rendering, and what it takes to do rendering of the web at scale. Take a
  look behind the scenes, and learn about some things a site owner could watch out for with
  regards to rendering.
* [Titles, snippets, and result previews](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ezLO7yC4aFo&list=PLKoqnv2vTMUO0F7xnv1MR2n-n7Y2CFgpR&index=5)
  - What's the goal of titles, snippets, and previews in Search? How do Google's systems pick and
  generate a preview for a page? What are some of the elements that help users decide which page
  to click in Search?
* [Googlebot and web hosting](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JvYh1oe5Zx0&list=PLKoqnv2vTMUO0F7xnv1MR2n-n7Y2CFgpR&index=6)
  - Starting with a look at the popularity of different web servers, and the growth of HTTPS,
  you'll find out more about how Google's crawling for Search works, and what you can do to
  control it.
* [Claim your Knowledge Panel](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l_Ws66DpJhE&list=PLKoqnv2vTMUO0F7xnv1MR2n-n7Y2CFgpR&index=7)
  - Knowledge Panels are a great way for people and organizations to be visible in Search. Find
  out more about the ways you can claim and update them for yourself or for your business.
* [Improving Search over the years](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DeW-9fhvkLM&list=PLKoqnv2vTMUO0F7xnv1MR2n-n7Y2CFgpR&index=8)
  - Are dogs the same as cats? Should pages about New York be shown when searching for York? How
  could algorithms ever figure this out? How many emoji's does it take to get Google's attention?
  Google's Paul Haahr takes you on a tour of some changes in Search.

We hope you find these videos insightful, useful, and a bit entertaining! And if you are not
subscribed to the Webmasters Youtube channel,
[here's your chance](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCWf2ZlNsCGDS89VBF_awNvA?sub_confirmation=1)!
